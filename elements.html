Hi!👋, Jones here. Thank you for taking the time to look at my portfolio. Here’s a little about me. I'm a curious problem-solver who believes in the power of data to tell compelling stories and spark smarter decisions. I thrive at the intersection of data, people, and purpose. Whether it’s uncovering insights from messy datasets or building dashboards that drive action, I’m always looking for better ways to ask questions and find answers. Over the years, I’ve worked on projects across diverse sectors including livelihoods, nutrition, youth development, environment, and humanitarian response. What excites me most is driving meaningful change—where data meets human impact, and numbers translate into real-world transformation. With a Master’s degree in International Relations, a Master’s degree in Rural Development and Extension, and an Agricultural Bachelor’s degree, I bridge global policy perspectives with on-the-ground development challenges. This unique academic blend equips me with both macro-level geopolitical perspectives and grassroots-level development expertise.

🧠my thinking process

1. Start upstream, not just in-depth: I map systems before solving problems.

2. Ask better questions: Before any tool or model, I focus on framing the problem right.

3. Clarity: I love building clean, intuitive visuals that even non-data folks can understand.

4. Learning never stops: I stay curious, whether it's learning a new tool or diving into behavior change theories.

🛠️favorite tools

1. Excel (yes, still powerful!)

3. R

Currently Exploring: Python (pandas, seaborn, scikit-learn), Machine Learning, AI for social impact, responsible data practices, and better ways to communicate uncertainty.
